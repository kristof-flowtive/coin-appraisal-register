What we built

A purpose-built iPad app that replaces your legacy spreadsheet workflow for bulk silver, gold, and platinum buys. Floor staff select themselves from a dropdown, add the coins or bullion in front of them, and tap Calculate Total to get an offer computed against the current market spot price and your margin policy.

The app is a Progressive Web App (PWA) — it lives at a web URL, but installs to the iPad home screen and runs full-screen, like a native app. No App Store approval, no distribution headache. Same URL, same install, on every iPad.

Single design contract: margins, the coin list, and the rep list all live in your Airtable base. You change a margin or add a rep in Airtable, and the next time floor staff tap Calculate, the new value is reflected. There is no developer in the loop for routine changes.

What's included

Engine 1 — Bulk Silver & Metals Calculator

  • Rep selector — staff pick themselves from a dropdown before pricing a deal. Switching reps mid-shift automatically clears any in-progress bag, so one rep's items can never carry into another rep's deal.
  • Coin & bullion picker — 24 coin types preloaded, grouped Silver / Gold / Platinum. Tap a coin, type the quantity (or weight in grams for bulk), tap Add to Bag.
  • Per-count and per-weight pricing — Roosevelt dimes, Morgan dollars, war nickels and the like price by count; .999 fine silver, 10K/14K/18K/22K gold, sterling, and platinum scrap price by gram.
  • Live spot prices — every calculation pulls the current market price for gold, silver, and platinum. Never cached. Spot is shown alongside the total so the rep sees the market context.
  • Per-category margin — your margin policy from Airtable is applied automatically; staff never see or touch the math.
  • Multi-line bag — add as many coins or bullion items as the customer brings; edit any line in place, remove any line individually, or start a fresh bag with a confirm prompt.
  • Crash-safe cart — if an iPad accidentally locks or the app is closed mid-deal, the bag is preserved when staff re-open the app. The selected rep persists too.
  • Clear error handling — if the iPad loses Wi-Fi or the pricing service has an issue, staff see an unmistakable message rather than a stale or zero total.
  • Refresh Config button — when admin adds a new coin type or rep in Airtable, staff tap Refresh Config and it shows up immediately. No app reinstall.

How a calculation actually works

Each tap of Calculate Total runs this end-to-end:

  1. The iPad sends the current bag (line by line) to a Make.com workflow.
  2. That workflow fetches today's spot prices for gold, silver, and platinum from metals-api.com.
  3. It reads your current margin percentages from Airtable (Config_Margins).
  4. It computes each line's offer and the grand total, and returns them to the iPad.

The market price you see on screen is the price at the moment of the tap. If you change a margin in Airtable while staff are using the app, the next Calculate reflects the new margin instantly — no app refresh required.

What admin can change without a developer

The app reads four config tables from your Airtable base. Edits to any of these are reflected on the iPads with no code change, no redeploy, and (for margins) no app refresh.

Airtable tableWhat you can change
Config_Margins The percentage of melt value you pay for each metal category. Edit a number, save the row — the next Calculate on any iPad uses the new value.
Config_CoinTypes The list of coin types in the Add Coin picker. Add a new row (with name, metal type, pricing-by, and the troy-ounces-per-unit invariant), set active = true, save. Tap Refresh Config on the iPad — the new coin is in the picker.
Config_Reps The list of reps in the dropdown. Add or remove rows, toggle active. Tap Refresh Config on the iPad to see the change.
Config_RawCoinOverrides Reserved for M2 (raw / antique coin lookup). Empty for now.

API keys (metals-api.com, and others as M2 lands) live in Make.com under your account once we transfer ownership — never in the app, never in your browser, never visible to staff.

What's not in this delivery

M1 covers the most common transaction type on the floor — bulk silver and metals. The remaining engines are scoped for M2:

  • Graded coin lookup — barcode-scan or manual entry of PCGS/NGC certificate numbers, returning grade, coin identity, and PCGS Price Guide value alongside the CDN Greysheet wholesale reference.
  • eBay comparable prices — for collectibles outside the metals/graded categories. Staff types an item name; the app returns a sold-price range from recent eBay listings.
  • Full deal log — completed deals written back to Airtable with rep, customer info, driver's-license capture (hard block if missing), spot prices at the time of deal, line items, total offer, acceptance, payment method, and cash received.
  • Staff authentication — required once the app starts writing deals back to Airtable. Today the rep dropdown identifies who's on the deal; M2 will add a proper login.
  • Your branding — the current app uses a placeholder gold "C" icon and the default app name "Coin Register". Once you provide a logo and accent color, those drop in as a follow-up update.

  5. iPad smoke test: install the app on one of your floor iPads (using the steps above) and run a few test calculations. We'll do a 15-minute walkthrough together to confirm everything feels right on the actual hardware before staff use it for live deals.

After those, ownership of the entire stack — repo, hosting, Make scenarios, Airtable, API keys — sits with you. M2 can begin whenever you're ready to scope it.
